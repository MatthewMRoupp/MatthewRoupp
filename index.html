Certifications

Credentials and verified learning milestones.

Why Certifications? A strong resume and cover letter are important, but words alone aren’t enough as you move up the career ladder. Employers want to see proof. They want to see commitment, growth, and verified effort - not just claims. I’m still working toward my degree, and that takes time. Certifications allow me to demonstrate structured learning and measurable progress while I continue building that foundation. They show that I’m actively developing real skills, not just talking about them. There are many certifications available, and I approach them strategically. I start with accessible and foundational options, then continue building toward more advanced credentials as my knowledge grows. Each one adds documented proof of progress and supports the hands-on projects in this portfolio. Certifications are not the end goal, they’re stepping stones. They reflect consistency, discipline, and a commitment to continuous improvement.
